EFFECTS ON THE VALLEY

COVID-19 has changed all our lives in so many ways. The Imperial Valley was affected severely being that we had the 5th most cases in all of California. Due to the high case rate, we have all been struggling. Some of us lost family, friends, or even jobs creating immense financial stress. Our current situation has forced education to adjust to online learning for the safety of students. Classes that are hands on or creatively based have become harder to teach because of social distancing. Socialization has come to a halt leaving people with the inability to express their interests and creativity with projects and clubs because they have been canceled. Students are having a hard time adapting to the online envirnment; thus, losing several opportunies and experiences. This competition is our way of trying to allow STEM students to be able to socialize while having fun and creating a game. Our goal is to give them the experience they’d originally be missing.

WHY A CODING COMPETITION

Our current situation has definitely changed a lot in our personal lives and our education system. One of which includes that sense of unity and socialization that in-person school provides for students. At the beginning of summer, I had the wonderful idea of putting together a science competition that was modified to our online learning. I fell in love with this idea more and more each day when I realized it would not only give our Imperial Valley coders an opportunity to be competitive but also give students the sense of unity and inclusion online schooling lacks. Since Computer Science competitions aren’t common in the valley, I would love to give our coders the chance to test their skills. I feel like if students were to be exposed to competitions, they would be more likely to have the courage to explore the career path. I am honored to have the opportunity to provide an inclusive event to passionate students around the Valley.
